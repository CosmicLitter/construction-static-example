The owners came to us with a simple brief: a home that disappears into the
landscape and still feels warm when the wind is howling at thirty below.

We worked with architect Laura Finch to orient the house around winter sun
and summer shade, with deep overhangs, triple-glazed glass, and a tight,
well-insulated envelope that pushes energy use toward net zero. The interior
pairs board-formed concrete with reclaimed fir and hand-troweled plaster —
materials that will only get better with age.
