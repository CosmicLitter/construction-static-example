summary: A full restoration of a 1910 farmhouse that had weathered a century of Montana winters — preserving everything worth saving and quietly upgrading everything that wasn't.
---

The house had beautiful bones and no insulation. We sistered joists,
straightened walls, and brought the original Douglas fir floors back to life,
then tucked modern mechanicals, plumbing, and a performance envelope behind
restored plaster walls.

The owners wanted a home that felt like their grandmother's — with a shower
that works and a kitchen they could actually cook in. We think we got there.
